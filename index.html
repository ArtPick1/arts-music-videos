2025 Releases

Are You Living a Lie?

“Are You Living a Lie” presents a musically driven, lyrical reflection on spiritual identity, the emptiness of worldly pursuits, and the invitation to surrender fully to Christ. While its delivery feels intimate and declarative, it is sung throughout with expressive phrasing—blending poetic lyricism and melodic cadence. Click to read more...
The song challenges listeners to examine whether their lives match the faith they profess. It calls out shallow belief, self-deception, and cultural religion that lacks true transformation. Inspired by 2 Corinthians 13:5, this piece acts as a mirror—urging the audience to test themselves and return to genuine surrender.

Not Just Another Pretty Voice

A call to shout in worship—TERUAH style. Loud, raw praise that breaks down walls (Psalm 100:1–2). Click to read more...
“Not Just Another Pretty Voice” was written to break the stereotype of sanitized, polished praise. It draws from the biblical call to shout joyfully to the Lord—not just sing nicely. With dynamic vocal energy and raw instrumentation, it invites listeners to worship God boldly, with soul and conviction, not just style.

Jesus or Bocephus

A response to cultural Christianity, calling out idolatry and urging surrender to Christ. Click to read more...
This song directly responds to the themes and tone of Kid Rock’s “Jesus and Bocephus.” Rather than mixing rebellion with religious language, “Jesus or Bocephus” confronts the idolatry of American individualism, country pride, and self-made religion. It asks bluntly: Are you really following Christ, or are you worshiping a god made in your own image? With a blues-rock groove and gritty vocals, the song challenges listeners to choose between the real Jesus and the symbols of a hollow faith. It’s not just critique—it’s a call to repentance and transformation.

We Can't Let Go

A cry to hold fast to the truth even when the world tempts us to give up. Click to read more...
“We Can’t Let Go” — a bold and urgent anthem calling believers to awaken from spiritual passivity. Inspired by Acts 17 and Ezekiel 33, the song issues a wake-up call to Christians who’ve grown silent in a dark and idol-filled world. It challenges the Church to be lightbearers, watchmen, and vocal heralds of truth—emphasizing that silence equals complicity and lost souls. It’s not just musical; it’s missional.
